üåà Every Baby is Unique

  • Milestone ranges are guidelines, not deadlines
  • Some babies excel in motor skills, others in communication
  • Premature babies use "corrected age" for milestones

‚ö†Ô∏è When to Seek Assessment (Red Flags)

  • By 3 months: No social smiles, not tracking moving objects, very stiff or very floppy
  • By 6 months: No rolling, no reaching for objects, no babbling sounds
  • By 9 months: No sitting independently, no babbling, not responding to name
  • By 12 months: Not pulling to stand, no gestures (pointing, waving), no single words

‚úÖ What Parents Can Do

  1. Responsive Parenting: Respond to cries and cues promptly (builds secure attachment)
  2. Tummy Time: Daily practice from newborn period (prevents flat head, builds strength)
  3. Talk & Sing: Constant narration of activities (language development)
  4. Read Together: Even newborns benefit from hearing language rhythms
  5. Safe Exploration: Baby-proof and allow freedom to move and explore
  6. Limit Screen Time: AAP/CPS recommend zero screens under 18 months (except video calls)

üë® Father-Baby Bonding (Non-Feeding)

  • Bath time routine
  • Baby-wearing during walks
  • Reading books and singing songs
  • Tummy time play sessions
