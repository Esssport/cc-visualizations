üéØ Timing & Long-Term Outcomes

Why Timing Matters

Research shows that the timing of surgical intervention significantly impacts long-term fertility and testicular health:

  • Surgery before 12 months: >95% chance of normal fertility in adulthood
  • Surgery between 12-18 months: Still excellent outcomes, slightly lower fertility rates
  • Surgery after 2 years: Increased risk of fertility issues and testicular damage
  • Untreated bilateral cryptorchidism: >90% infertility rate

Key Takeaway: Early surgical intervention (ideally before 12 months, but no later than 18 months) provides the best long-term outcomes.

Benefits of Early Treatment
  • Preserves normal testicular function and fertility potential
  • Reduces risk of testicular cancer (though absolute risk remains low)
  • Allows for normal hormone production during development
  • Prevents psychological issues related to genital appearance
  • Enables easier self-examination for testicular health in adulthood
Post-Surgery Expectations
  • Short hospital stay (often same-day or overnight)
  • Quick recovery period (1-2 weeks for normal activity)
  • Minimal scarring with modern techniques
  • Follow-up appointments to ensure testicles remain in position
  • Normal physical development and puberty expected
