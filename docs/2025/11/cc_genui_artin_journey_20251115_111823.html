Stage 2: Early Infancy (1-3 Months)

Upcoming

The "Fourth Trimester" - Adaptation Phase

Physical Milestones
  • Neck strength: Lifts head 45¬∞ during tummy time by 2 months
  • Hand control: Begins batting at objects, brings hands to mouth
  • Vision: Tracks moving objects, focuses on faces at 12+ inches
  • Growth: Gains 5-7 oz per week, grows 1-1.5 inches per month
Cognitive & Social Leaps
  • First social smile (6-8 weeks) - deliberate response to your face
  • Coos and makes vowel sounds ("ahhh", "ohhh")
  • Recognizes parents' faces and prefers them to strangers
  • Shows excitement by wiggling arms and legs
  • Begins to self-soothe (finding thumb, looking at hands)
Sleep Development
  • Total sleep: 14-16 hours (starting to consolidate at night)
  • Night sleep: 8-10 hours with 2-3 wakings for feeds
  • Naps: 3-4 naps per day (30 min - 2 hours each)
  • Wake windows: 60-90 minutes between naps
Feeding Changes
  • Frequency: 7-9 feeds per day (stretching between feeds)
  • Amount: 4-6 oz per bottle feed (if formula feeding)
  • Feeding cues: More predictable hunger signals
  • Growth spurt: Expect cluster feeding around 6 weeks and 3 months
Parent Care Priorities
  1. Tummy time: Start with 3-5 minutes, 2-3x daily (builds neck strength)
  2. Talk and sing: Narrate daily activities to build language foundation
  3. Create bedtime routine: Bath, feed, song, sleep (consistency helps)
  4. Watch for postpartum mood - Screen for PPD/PPND (EPDS screening)
  5. Introduce vitamin D - 400 IU daily if breastfeeding (Health Canada)
Doctor Visits
  • 2-month checkup: First vaccinations (DTaP, IPV, Hib, PCV, Rotavirus)

Red Flags to Watch

  • No social smile by 8 weeks
  • Doesn't track objects with eyes
  • Fever >100.4¬∞F (call doctor immediately at this age)

Stage 3: Developing Personality (3-6 Months)

Upcoming

The "Golden Age" - Predictable & Delightful

Physical Milestones
  • Rolling: Front to back (4 months), back to front (5-6 months)
  • Sitting: Sits with support (4 months), sits independently (6 months)
  • Reaching: Deliberately grabs toys, transfers objects hand to hand
  • Mouthing: Everything goes in the mouth (oral exploration phase)
  • Growth: Doubles birth weight by 5-6 months
Cognitive & Social Leaps
  • First laugh (3-4 months) - genuine belly laughs at peek-a-boo
  • Recognizes own name when spoken
  • Shows distinct preferences for people and toys
  • Babbles consonant-vowel combinations ("ba-ba", "ma-ma")
  • Understands cause and effect (shaking rattle makes sound)
  • Stranger awareness begins (prefers familiar faces)
Sleep Development
  • Total sleep: 12-15 hours (most at night!)
  • Night sleep: 10-12 hours (may sleep through by 6 months)
  • Naps: 3 naps per day (morning, afternoon, early evening)
  • Wake windows: 1.5-2.5 hours
  • Sleep training option: Can begin gentle methods after 4 months (if desired)
Feeding Changes
  • Solids introduction: Start around 6 months (signs of readiness):
    • Sits with minimal support
    • Lost tongue-thrust reflex
    • Shows interest in food
    • Can bring objects to mouth
  • Milk feeds: Still primary nutrition (breast or formula)
  • Amount: 6-8 oz per bottle, 5-6 times daily
Parent Care Priorities
  1. Create safe play space - Baby-proofing begins (rolling babies move!)
  2. Read daily - High-contrast books, board books with textures
  3. Prepare for solids - Iron-fortified cereal, pur√©es, or baby-led weaning
  4. Encourage reaching - Place toys slightly out of reach to motivate
  5. Respond to babbling - Conversation turns build language skills
Doctor Visits
  • 4-month checkup: Vaccinations (DTaP, IPV, Hib, PCV, Rotavirus)
  • 6-month checkup: Vaccinations, developmental screening, discuss solids

What to Look Forward To

  • Sitting independently - changes perspective on the world
  • First foods - messy, hilarious, camera-ready moments
  • Longer night sleep stretches - some babies sleep 10+ hours!

Stage 4: Mobility Emerges (6-9 Months)

Upcoming

The Explorer - Everything is Fascinating

Physical Milestones
  • Crawling: Army crawl (7 months), hands-and-knees crawl (8-9 months)
  • Standing: Pulls to stand with support (8-9 months)
  • Pincer grasp: Picks up small objects with thumb and forefinger
  • Teeth: First teeth appear (usually lower front incisors, 6-10 months)
  • Growth: Gains 3-5 oz per week (slower than early months)
Cognitive & Social Leaps
  • Object permanence - understands objects exist when hidden (peek-a-boo becomes hilarious)
  • Responds to own name consistently
  • Shows separation anxiety (healthy attachment sign)
  • Imitates sounds and gestures (waves bye-bye, claps)
  • Understands "no" (even if doesn't always obey!)
  • Points at objects of interest
Sleep Development
  • Total sleep: 12-14 hours
  • Night sleep: 11-12 hours (usually consolidated)
  • Naps: Transitioning from 3 to 2 naps (morning and afternoon)
  • Wake windows: 2.5-3.5 hours
  • Sleep regression: Common around 8-10 months (practice new skills at night)
Feeding Changes
  • Solids: 2-3 meals per day, variety of textures
  • Self-feeding: Finger foods, learning to use spoon (messy!)
  • Milk: 24-32 oz formula or 4-6 breastfeeds per day
  • Water: Introduce sippy cup with water at meals
  • Allergen introduction: Introduce peanuts, eggs, dairy (early = protective)
Parent Care Priorities
  1. Baby-proof thoroughly - Outlet covers, cabinet locks, stair gates
  2. Encourage crawling - Place toys out of reach, create crawling obstacle course
  3. Practice self-feeding - Offer soft finger foods (banana, avocado, steamed veggies)
  4. Narrate everything - "I'm putting the cup on the table" builds vocabulary
  5. Establish dental care - Wipe gums/teeth with soft cloth after feeds
Doctor Visits
  • 9-month checkup: Developmental screening, lead and iron screening (if needed)

Red Flags to Watch

  • No babbling by 9 months
  • Doesn't respond to name
  • No weight-bearing on legs when supported

Stage 5: Almost a Toddler (9-12 Months)

Upcoming

The Communicator - Understanding So Much

Physical Milestones
  • Cruising: Walks while holding furniture (9-11 months)
  • Standing alone: Stands without support (10-12 months)
  • First steps: Some babies walk by 12 months (range is 9-18 months)
  • Stacking: Stacks 2 blocks, puts objects in containers
  • Teeth: 4-8 teeth by first birthday
Cognitive & Social Leaps
  • First words: "Mama", "Dada" used meaningfully (10-12 months)
  • Follows simple commands ("Come here", "Give me the ball")
  • Understands 50+ words even if can't say them
  • Shows preferences and opinions (shakes head "no")
  • Plays interactive games (patty-cake, peek-a-boo)
  • Tests boundaries (throws food to see reaction)
Sleep Development
  • Total sleep: 12-14 hours
  • Night sleep: 11-12 hours (hopefully uninterrupted!)
  • Naps: 2 naps (some transition to 1 nap late in this stage)
  • Wake windows: 3-4 hours
  • 12-month sleep regression: Common as brain develops rapidly
Feeding Changes
  • Solids: 3 meals + 2 snacks, eating many family foods
  • Self-feeding: Uses fingers, attempts spoon/fork (messy but learning!)
  • Milk: 16-24 oz formula or 3-4 breastfeeds
  • Transition planning: Can switch to whole milk at 12 months (if desired)
  • Hydration: 4-6 oz water throughout the day
Parent Care Priorities
  1. Support standing/walking - Push toys, hold hands for walking practice
  2. Read interactive books - Lift-the-flap, touch-and-feel books
  3. Name everything - "That's a dog! The dog says woof!" (label + sound)
  4. Allow safe exploration - Create "yes spaces" where baby can explore freely
  5. Plan first birthday - Simple celebration, smash cake photos!
Doctor Visits
  • 12-month checkup: Vaccinations (MMR, Varicella, Hep A), developmental screening

What to Look Forward To

  • First birthday celebration - YOU made it through the first year!
  • First words - hearing "mama" or "dada" intentionally is magical
  • First steps - the beginning of true independence
  • More consistent sleep - many 12-month-olds sleep through the night
